# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two audiences arriving with different mindsets, neither clearly dominant:

- **Commercial clients** — property managers, office managers, hotel and hospitality buyers evaluating vendors, comparing options, and building internal business cases. They need to feel confident in the quality and reliability of the work before reaching out.
- **Residential clients** — homeowners and interior design enthusiasts who want to bring nature into their living spaces. They arrive more emotionally than analytically, drawn by beauty and possibility.

Both groups are making a considered decision, not an impulse purchase. They're assessing trust as much as aesthetics.

## Product Purpose

Interior Greenscapes is a professional interior plantscaping and green wall design business serving commercial and residential spaces throughout the Treasure Valley (Boise, Idaho area). With 30+ years of experience and 500+ completed projects, the site's job is to convert visitors into consultation requests — by demonstrating expertise through the portfolio, communicating warmth and approachability through the brand, and making the next step frictionless.

## Brand Personality

**Warm, approachable, natural.** Inviting and human. Plants are a joy, not a status symbol. Friendly expertise without intimidation. 30 years of experience earns quiet authority, but the voice stays personal and grounded.

## Anti-references

- **Generic landscaping company** — stock photos of lawnmowers, clip-art trees, cheesy taglines, and the visual language of every local lawn-care site.
- **Cluttered local business** — busy layouts, low contrast, too many competing calls to action, hard-to-navigate pages, a site that feels like it hasn't been reconsidered in a decade.

## Design Principles

1. **Let the work speak** — The portfolio and photography carry the real weight. Design steps back so installations tell their own story; chrome and decoration don't compete with the plants.
2. **Quiet credibility** — 30 years of expertise doesn't need to announce itself. Trust is built through clarity, restraint, and specificity — not superlatives or hero metrics.
3. **Living warmth** — Every surface should feel organic. Warm tints over cold whites; soft curves over hard corners; unhurried spacing over compressed density.
4. **Natural rhythm** — Layout, typography, and spacing should breathe like a space that's been thoughtfully planted. Nothing crammed; nothing shouting.
5. **Local and specific** — This is a Treasure Valley business with real relationships and a real community. The site should feel human and rooted, not mass-market or franchise-generic.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as baseline. Prioritize readable body type (Lora at comfortable sizes), sufficient contrast on green-over-green combinations, and reduced-motion respect for animation.
